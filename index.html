The Iron Yard

I am a graduate of The Iron Yard, Indianapolis, where I spent 4 months being trained full-time as a front- and back-end developer. A couple of the projects that I enjoyed working on are included in this portfolio, but most of my work is on GitHub. I also have a resume attached, if you would like to take a look.

Virginia Tech

Prior to coming to The Iron Yard, I was trained as a biochemist, with a focus on gene analysis and other bioinformatics methods.

At Virginia Tech, I dedicated my time to research. I worked at a protein simulations lab and investigated aggregation interactions of toxic species of proteins involved in Alzheimer's disease and Type II Diabetes. I was also working at a tissue regeneration lab, at the cross-section of biopolymer engineering and additive manufacturing, where I worked with UV-curable polymers and tested their biocompatibility on immortalized mouse bone tissue cells. These polymers were used for microstereolithographic printing (3D printing technique) of tissue scaffolds with intricate microarchitecture that were intended to assist the body in rebuilding its own tissues. (I promise I don't speak like this in person :D)

That was all to say that I love research, technology, and can get pretty nostalgic about it --

When it was time for me to decide where I would like to go next, I came to this conclusion: I could either continue doing research, narrow my focus on a particular scientific riddle, and attempt to solve it, or I could learn to build software and create tools to help others solve riddles of their own (and within my lifetime). I chose the latter, and I chose the opportunity to one day share neat things about science with a wider audience in a not-research-journals way. This motivation brought me to The Iron Yard, where I learned to translate research and problem solving skills into code.
